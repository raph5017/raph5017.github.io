Pothole Database in Cheltenham, UK

Introduction & Methods

The aim of this study was to use PostgreSQL to create and interrogate a database of pothole locations, similar to what a local authority would need. Non-spatial tables include pothole details and employee information. The spatial tables include a point table showing the location of potholes, two tables consisting of line data representing roads and bike lanes, and a table containing polygon data of different wards within the town. There are no attribute relationships between the road, bike lanes and wards tables, however, they share a spatial relationship that they also share with the pothole location table. This allows for spatial queries using PostGIS.
